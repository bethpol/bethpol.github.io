Cornell Rocketry Team

Electrical Subteam (October 2021-Present)

The Cornell Rocketry Team is an undergraduate project team comprised of about 30 engineers that designs, prototypes, and builds a high-powered rocket each year to compete in the Spaceport America Cup intercollegiate competition. I decided to join Rocketry my freshman year to develop my engineering skills within this collaborative, dynamic, and fun team environment. My responsibilities so far have included:

  • Trained in Altium, an industry standard software for schematic and PCB design
  • Complete a semester-long onboarding project to design an FSM display board PCB
  • Assist with soldering and testing the payload deployment system
  • Attend design reviews and all-team meetings to famiarize myself with existing systems in the rocket

W2CXM: Cornell Amateur Radio Club

Club Member (August 2021-Present)

One of the oldest active clubs at Cornell, W2CXM is involved in traditional amateur radio club activities and also works on weather balloon launches that employ radio technology for communication between the payload and ground station. After first learning about this group at our school's clubfest, I decided to join and am now working towards my technician certification. I am greatly interested by the history of amateur radio as well as the technology behind it. For our weather balloon project, I am developing a payload deployment board for our upcoming launch!
