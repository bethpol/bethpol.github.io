HAB Launches

High-Altitude Balloon Launches with Amatuer Radio Club

As a member of the Cornell University Amateur Radio Club (W2CXM), I have contributed to payload development and design for two high-altitude balloon (HAB) launches, occuring in Spring 2022 and Fall 2022 respectively. Our HAB launches are filled with helium and ascend to a height of 30,000 meters before bursting and descending. The goal of these launches is to serve as a testbed for research projects carried out by labs at Cornell, including the Space Systems Design Studio's Alpha Cubesat. W2CXM members design and implement a tracking system for the balloon, and we attatch payloads from the labs to the main tracking system.

For these launches, our tracking systems operate on the 900 MHz band and utilize LoRa. The main flight code loop runs on a T-Beam. In addition, GPS data is transmitted via 4-FSK.

For the Spring 2022 launch, I assisted with flight code development, and created and soldered a board to cut down a payload mid-flight. The goal of this board was to test our ability to establish an uplink and use it to trigger an event mid-flight (cutting down a payload). My board uses a MOSFET and nichrome wire to burn off a wire when the signal is received.

For the Fall 2022 launch, I created a second version of my original board, and assisted with the integration of cameras to the payload box.
